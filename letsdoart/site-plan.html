Overview

Purpose

The purpose of the website will be to invite neighborhood residents to share their artistic gifts and talents through an art competition in two different areas. We seek to give amateur people with limited resources the opportunity to win cash prizes and the possibility of participating in national and provincial competitions.

Audience

This art competition will be aimed at people over 18 years of age, who are not art professionals and who have not participated in recognized competitions.

Content

Home page

The idea or concept of this site is to present an organized and formal page of a neighborhood committee (in this case Villa Galicia) so that the neighbors have a commonplace and can have access to all the proposals that the committee offers. This will be done so that people can show their art, offer their services, find resources to improve their economic situation, with courses, workshops, etc., and have the possibility of finding a place of support and help. The central idea is to “help each other in an organized way.” We try to offer as easy as possible access to all the options that the committee offers so that everyone can find the right place they are looking for on the website. These options include recreation, artistic events, courses, workshops, services, a job board, a question-and-answer blog, and a support forum. In this case, the website will be inaugurated with an art competition (for people over 18 years of age) and a fair for artisans and micro-entrepreneurs. “Art is an activity where a man can recreate part of his reality, his feelings, emotions, ideas, and beauty. With art we create catharsis, we open ourselves. Art is therapeutic, and liberating. Art helps us strengthen self-esteem, and self-confidence, and connect with others. It gives us peace, reduces stress, enhances our memory, and improves our concentration.”

Art Competition The contest will be aimed at those neighbors who dedicate themselves to artistic painting and modeling on an amateur basis and give them the opportunity to compete for cash, credits, and the possibility of competing at the national level. This will be an incentive for those who, due to low economic or social resources (many contests are paid or directed to important people) do not have or have access to progress in their art

Crafts Fair and Buffet For neighbors who have other gifts and talents and want to show them or have the possibility of selling them, there will be an artisan fair where they will be given a space to exhibit their entrepreneurship or art. The authorized items will be those that are 70% handmade with any type of materials and that maintain an appropriate code of decency. No food will be allowed, except cookies, puddings, cakes, and preserves (for more information, contact the contact methods suggested on the website). The committee will provide a buffet with food and drinks for sale throughout the weekend. The proceeds will be donated to charities and community kitchens in the neighborhood.
